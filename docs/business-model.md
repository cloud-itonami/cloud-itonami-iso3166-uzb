# Business model — UZB

Independent public-sector market-entry compliance for the Republic of
Uzbekistan.

- Public procurement is governed by Decree of the President of the
  Republic of Uzbekistan No. PP-4544 (5 December 2019) "On measures to
  further improve public procurement system and to broadly involve
  business entities in public procurement process" (own text confirmed
  directly on gov.uz's official page). `xarid.uzex.uz` is the live,
  independently-confirmed information portal. This iteration could not
  independently confirm or rule out a separate parliamentary "Law on
  Public Procurement" this session, nor a single named procurement
  regulator -- both honestly disclosed gaps (see
  `src/marketentry/facts.cljc`).
- STIR (taxpayer identification number) tax registration is
  administered by the State Tax Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan
  (`soliq.uz`) -- confirmed via that body's own live "Stiringizni
  aniqlang" (Determine your STIR) service (via a Wayback Machine
  snapshot, since this session's own live connection to soliq.uz timed
  out at the TCP level).
- Business registration runs through the `birdarcha.uz` single-window
  front-end, directly hyperlinked from gov.uz's own official "State
  registration of business entities" page as "the system" for
  independent registration, alongside an in-person "Public Services
  Center" channel. This iteration could not independently confirm which
  specific ministry/agency legally administers `birdarcha.uz` -- an
  honest gap.
- Foreign investment is governed by the Law "On investments and
  investment activity", No. LRU-598 (adopted by the Legislative Chamber
  9 December 2019, approved by the Senate 14 December 2019) -- Article
  21 bars nationalization and requires compensation "adequate to
  inflicted loss" for extraordinary-circumstance expropriation; Article
  15 guarantees non-discrimination against investors. Article 26 names
  the authorized state body as the "Ministry of investments and foreign
  trade" -- but this iteration found a genuine RENAME: the live
  Investment Promotion Agency's own site (`invest.gov.uz`) now
  self-describes as operating under the "Ministry of Investment,
  Industry, and Trade", confirming the name has changed since the 2019
  law's own text (no explicit renaming decree/date was found this
  session, an honestly unresolved rename date).
- Labour Code of the Republic of Uzbekistan (adopted 28 October 2022)
  Article 130 sets a category-dependent statutory ceiling on the
  duration of an initial probation period: 3 months for general
  employees, 6 months for heads of organizations and their deputies,
  chief accountants, and heads of separate divisions of organizations
  -- this vertical's flagship governor check.
